Crispy Fried Rice with Cucumber Salad

This is better (and quicker) with leftover rice that you've kept in the fridge for a day, but this recipe uses fresh rice.

Serves
2
Cooking time
1hr

Ingredients

  • 175g basmati rice (or 500g leftover cooked rice)
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp vegetable or sunflower oil
  • 6 spring onions, finely sliced
  • 1 garlic cloves, peeled and crushed
  • 1 red chilli, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 1/2 tsp caster sugar
  • 2 tbsp lime juice (ie, from 1 lime)
  • 1 1/2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1/2 cucumber, deseeded and diced
  • 75g edamame beans (optional)
  • 25g salted peanuts, roughly chopped
  • small handful coriander leaves, roughly chopped
  • small handful mint leaves, roughly chopped

Method

  1. If you have leftover rice, skip straight to step 9
  2. Rinse rice in cold tap until water runs clear
  3. Add to 300ml boiling water in a pan
  4. Bring to the boil and then turn down to the lowest possible heat
  5. Put the lid on and cook for 15 minutes
  6. Turn heat off and leave to steam for a further 5 minutes
  7. Spread out onto plates and leave to cool completely
  8. Put in a tupperware in the fridge until you need it
  9. In a large pan that has a lid, warm the oil over a medium-high heat
  10. Once hot, add the spring onions and fry while stirring for two minutes
  11. Add the rice and salt and mix
  12. Press the rice mixture down with a large spatula or fish slice so it is level all over and covering the base of the pan
  13. Cook for 7 minutes, pushing the rice down now and then
  14. Meanwhile, start making the salad by mixing garlic, chilli, sugar and lime juice in a bowl
  15. Add the cucumber, edamame beans, soy saucen, peanuts and herbs to the bowl and toss it about
  16. Flip the rice in chunks using the spatula and cook for another 7 minutes
  17. Repeat this process until the rice turns golden brown (probably once more either side)
  18. To serve, spread the rice onto plates and cover with the salad in the centre
  19. Eat straight away
